JEE Mains Mathematics Syllabus

Class 11 (Total weightage: 25-30%)

  • Sets, Relations, and Functions: 2-3%
  • Complex Numbers and Quadratic Equations: 3-4%
  • Matrices and Determinants: 7-8%
  • Permutations and Combinations: 4-5%
  • Mathematical Induction: 1-2%
  • Binomial Theorem: 2-3%
  • Sequences and Series: 4-5%

Class 12 (Total weightage: 70-75%)

  • Relations and Functions: 2-3%
  • Inverse Trigonometric Functions: 3-4%
  • Matrices: 3-4%
  • Determinants: 3-4%
  • Continuity and Differentiability: 8-9%
  • Applications of Derivatives: 3-4%
  • Integrals: 9-10%
  • Applications of Integrals: 3-4%
  • Differential Equations: 6-7%
  • Vector Algebra: 6-7%
  • Three Dimensional Geometry: 4-5%
  • Linear Programming: 1-2%
  • Probability: 6-7%

It is important to note that the weightage can vary every year and these are just approximate values. Therefore, it is recommended to cover all the topics thoroughly to increase the chances of scoring well in JEE Main Physics.
