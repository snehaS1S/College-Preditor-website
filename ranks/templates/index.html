JEE Mains: Everything You Need to Know

If you're planning to pursue a career in engineering, the Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) Mains is a must-take exam. Conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA), JEE Mains is a computer-based exam that assesses a candidate's knowledge in physics, chemistry, and mathematics.

The JEE Mains exam is conducted twice a year, in January and April, and candidates can choose to appear for either or both exams. The exam is divided into two papers â€“ Paper 1 for engineering aspirants and Paper 2 for architecture and planning aspirants.

The JEE Mains exam is a highly competitive exam, with lakhs of candidates appearing for it every year. To crack the exam, candidates need to have a strong foundation in the subjects covered and should be well-versed in problem-solving and time management skills.

JEE Mains Syllabus

The JEE Mains syllabus is based on the NCERT curriculum for classes 11 and 12. The topics covered in the exam include:

  • Physics - Mechanics, Thermodynamics, Electromagnetism, Optics, and Modern PhysicsMore Details
  • Chemistry - Physical Chemistry, Organic Chemistry, and Inorganic ChemistryMore Details
  • Mathematics - Algebra, Trigonometry, Coordinate Geometry, Calculus, and VectorsMore Details

JEE Mains Preparation Tips

Preparing for the JEE Mains exam requires dedication and hard work. Here are some tips that can help you crack the exam:

  • Have a clear understanding of the syllabus and exam pattern
  • Make a study plan and stick to it
  • Practice as many sample papers and mock tests as possible
  • Revise regularly and focus on your weak areas
  • Stay calm and focused during the exam

Frequently Asked Questions

What is JEE Mains?

JEE Mains is a national level engineering entrance exam conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) for admission into undergraduate engineering courses in various institutes in India.

What is JEE Advanced?

JEE Advanced is a national level engineering entrance exam conducted by one of the IITs for admission into undergraduate engineering courses in the IITs and some other premier institutes in India.

What is the eligibility criteria for JEE Mains?

The eligibility criteria for JEE Mains include a minimum of 75% marks in 12th board exams or being in the top 20 percentile in the respective board exams. There is also a relaxation of 5% marks for SC/ST candidates.

What is the eligibility criteria for JEE Advanced?

Only those candidates who clear the JEE Mains and are in the top 2,50,000 rank holders are eligible to appear for JEE Advanced.

What is the exam pattern for JEE Mains?

JEE Mains consists of multiple choice questions (MCQs) and numerical value-based questions (NVBs) in subjects like Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. The exam duration is 3 hours.

What is the exam pattern for JEE Advanced?

JEE Advanced consists of two papers - Paper 1 and Paper 2. Both papers have objective-type and numerical answer-type questions in subjects like Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. The exam duration for each paper is 3 hours.

What is the syllabus for JEE Mains?

The syllabus for JEE Mains includes topics from Class 11 and Class 12 Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.

What is the syllabus for JEE Advanced?

The syllabus for JEE Advanced is similar to JEE Mains but includes additional topics and is more conceptually advanced.

Can I appear for both JEE Mains and Advanced?

Yes, you can appear for both exams provided you meet the eligibility criteria for both.

How are the ranks for JEE Mains and Advanced calculated?

The ranks for JEE Mains are calculated based on the normalized score obtained in the exam. The ranks for JEE Advanced are calculated based on the aggregate marks obtained in both Paper 1 and Paper 2.

What are some good books for JEE preparation?

Some popular books for JEE preparation include NCERT textbooks, H.C. Verma for Physics, Cengage for Mathematics, and Morrison and Boyd for Chemistry.

How can I check my JEE Mains and Advanced results?

You can check your JEE Mains and Advanced results on the official websites of the respective exams.
